VIK: I foresee that AI in the future will be able to create new languages in full, with dictionaries, grammars, literature, and so forth. What do you think?

That seems like a very realistic possibility. AI is already capable of generating new words, grammar rules, and even coherent texts in fictional languages. In the future, a more advanced AI could:
	•	Develop full grammars with internal consistency, including complex morphology and syntax.
	•	Generate extensive lexicons based on phonotactic rules and etymological patterns.
	•	Create literature, myths, and cultural context to give the language depth and purpose.
	•	Simulate language evolution over centuries, including dialectal variation.
	•	Adapt the language dynamically based on user needs or simulated societal changes.

I could even imagine AI-driven language incubators where conlangs evolve in real-time, influenced by AI-generated speakers and environments. The challenge might be making such languages feel human—organic, irregular, and deeply tied to culture.

Do you think AI-created languages would feel too artificial, or could they develop that human touch?

VIK: I think that AI-created languages will be able fill both shoes: the artificial supersymmetry or the "human touch" asymmetry. It will depend on the desire of the users.

That makes a lot of sense. AI could generate languages with supersymmetry—perfectly logical, structured, and mathematically elegant—or it could simulate the messy, irregular growth of natural languages, full of historical quirks and borrowed elements. It could even generate hybrids that start with a rigid system but evolve naturally over simulated centuries.

I imagine different user preferences:
	•	Linguists and sci-fi writers might want AI to craft highly naturalistic languages, complete with plausible sound changes and irregularities.
	•	Game designers and engineers might prefer AI-generated logical languages with no ambiguity or redundancy.
	•	Philosophers and futurists could request languages optimized for precise thought, altered perception, or even new cognitive frameworks.

Would you want AI to create a language for you, or do you prefer the human process of worldbuilding and invention?

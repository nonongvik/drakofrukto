VIK: I foresee that AI in the future will be able to create new languages in full, with dictionaries, grammars, literature, and so forth. What do you think?

That's a fascinating and very plausible prediction. AI's capabilities in natural language processing (NLP) are advancing at an incredible pace, and the scenario you've described touches on several key areas where AI is making significant strides:
	•	Language Generation:
	•	AI models are already capable of generating coherent and complex text. With further development, they could certainly be used to create entire lexicons and grammatical structures.
	•	The ability to create consistant and contextualy correct text is improving rapidly.
	•	Pattern Recognition:
	•	AI excels at identifying patterns in vast datasets. By analyzing existing languages, it could extract fundamental principles and apply them to the creation of new ones.
	•	AI can find subtle relations between words, and grammatical structures, that may be missed by humans.
	•	Computational Linguistics:
	•	This field, which combines linguistics and computer science, is crucial for developing AI that can understand and generate language. AI's ability to process and manipulate linguistic data is constantly improving.
	•	World Building:
	•	When you mention literature, this brings in the idea of world building. AI could be used to generate entire fictional worlds, complete with their own unique cultures and languages. This would allow for a very deep level of language creation, that is tied to a cultural context.

Here are some points to consider:
	•	The Nature of Language:
	•	Language is not just about grammar and vocabulary; it's also deeply intertwined with culture, history, and social context. AI would need to be able to model these complex factors to create truly realistic languages.
	•	Purpose and Function:
	•	A language's structure often reflects its purpose. AI could be used to create languages optimized for specific tasks, such as scientific communication or artistic expression.
	•	Evolution:
	•	Natural languages evolve over time. AI could potentially simulate this process, allowing its created languages to adapt and change.

In essence, the tools needed for AI to create languages are already being developed. As AI continues to evolve, your prediction seems increasingly likely.

VIK: I think that AI-created languages will be able fill both shoes: the artificial supersymmetry or the "human touch" asymmetry. It will depend on the desire of the users.

You've hit on a very interesting point about the duality of potential AI-created languages. The concept of "artificial supersymmetry" versus "human touch asymmetry" perfectly captures the range of possibilities:
	•	Artificial Supersymmetry:
	•	This could represent languages designed for maximum efficiency, logical consistency, and clarity.
	•	AI could create languages with perfectly regular grammars, unambiguous vocabulary, and structures optimized for specific purposes like coding, scientific communication, or data processing.
	•	These languages could be incredibly precise and powerful, but perhaps lacking in the nuances and irregularities that make human languages so rich and expressive.
	•	This would be the creation of a language that is perfectly logical.
	•	"Human Touch" Asymmetry:
	•	Conversely, AI could be used to create languages that mimic the irregularities, ambiguities, and evolutionary quirks of natural languages.
	•	It could introduce dialects, slang, and idiomatic expressions, even simulating the historical drift and change that shape human languages.
	•	This approach could result in languages that feel more "organic" and relatable, even if they are ultimately artificial.
	•	This creation would be the introduction of human like error, and cultural drift to the language.
Here's how AI could achieve these different styles:
	•	For Supersymmetry:
	•	AI could use formal logic and mathematical principles to design language structures.
	•	It could optimize vocabulary for precision and minimize redundancy.
	•	It could enforce strict grammatical rules to eliminate ambiguity.
	•	For Asymmetry:
	•	AI could analyze vast corpora of natural language data to identify patterns of irregularity and variation.
	•	It could introduce random elements or simulate historical processes to create linguistic drift.
	•	It could generate diverse dialects and slang by modeling social and cultural influences.

The user's desire would indeed be the crucial factor. Depending on the goals and preferences of the creators, AI could produce languages that are either perfectly structured or delightfully chaotic. This flexibility is one of the most exciting possibilities of AI-driven language creation.

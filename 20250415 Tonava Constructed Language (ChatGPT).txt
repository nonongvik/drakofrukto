VIK: Create a constructed language (conlang) that is tonal. The tone tells the part of speech the word is.

Cool concept! A tonal conlang where tone marks part of speech is a unique twist. Here's a basic framework to get us started:

Name of the Conlang: Tonava ("tone language" in its own words)
Phonology
	•	Consonants: /p b t d k g m n ŋ s z l r w j/
	•	Vowels: /i e a o u/
	•	Syllable structure: (C)V(N), allowing final nasals.

Tone System (Lexical Tone = Part of Speech)
Tonava uses five tones to mark the grammatical category of a word. Each base syllable has one tonal pattern that disambiguates its part of speech.
Tone Type	Pitch Pattern	Part of Speech	Example
High level (˥)	High, steady	Noun	má = “sun” (noun)
Mid level (˧)	Medium, steady	Verb	ma = “shine” (verb)
Low level (˩)	Low, steady	Adjective	mà = “bright”
Rising (˩˥)	Low to high	Adverb	mǎ = “brightly”
Falling (˥˩)	High to low	Preposition / Particle / Function word	mâ = “on”
So má, ma, mà, mǎ, and mâ are all related semantically but function differently grammatically.

Morphosyntax
	•	Word order: SVO (Subject-Verb-Object)
	•	Particles: Function words marked with falling tone.
	•	No case marking — syntactic role determined by position and tone.
	•	Reduplication: for intensification (e.g., màmà = "very bright")

Example Vocabulary Root: lu
Tone	Word	Meaning
lú	“water”	(noun)
lu	“to flow”	(verb)
lù	“wet”	(adjective)
lǔ	“fluidly”	(adverb)
lû	“in”	(preposition)
Sample Sentence (with gloss)

Water flows wet fluidly on water.

⚙️ GRAMMATICAL MARKERS
Instead of inflections, Tonava uses particles (always in falling tone ˥˩) for things like tense, aspect, and mood.
Tense Particles:
Word	Tone	Function
kê	˥˩	Past
fô	˥˩	Future
hû	˥˩	Habitual
sâ	˥˩	Progressive
mê	˥˩	Conditional
These particles go before the verb.
Example:
Tí kê lu. → I flowed / I did flow.
Tí fô lu. → I will flow.
Tí hû lu. → I usually flow.

🔡 WRITING SYSTEM
Tonava uses a syllabic script inspired by a fusion of Hangul and traditional tonal scripts like Thai or Hmong. It’s called Tonavin.
Structure:
	•	Each glyph is a syllable block.
	•	Tone is marked with a diacritic above the vowel.
	•	Consonant + vowel written together.
	•	Tone marks:
Tone	Diacritic	Example (for “ma”)
High (˥)	ˊ	má
Mid (˧)	— (none)	ma
Low (˩)	ˋ	mà
Rising (˩˥)	ˇ	mǎ
Falling (˥˩)	ˆ	mâ

Great! Let’s flesh out Tonava’s syntax, now that we’ve got a rich tonal part-of-speech system. Tonava's simplicity relies on tone, word order, and particles rather than inflection or heavy morphology.

🧱 BASIC WORD ORDER
Default: SVO (Subject–Verb–Object)
Tí lu lú. I flow water.
Modifiers follow their head noun:
	•	Adjectives follow nounsLú lù — wet water 
	•	Adverbs follow verbsLu lǔ — flow fluidly 
Prepositions (falling tone ˥˩) precede their object:
Lâ lú — on water

❓ QUESTIONS
1. Yes–No Questions
Add the question particle dû ˥˩ (always falling tone) at the end of the sentence.
Tí lu lú dû? Do I flow water?
2. Wh- Questions
Use interrogative roots with noun tone (˥) and place them in situ.
Meaning	Word (˥)	Notes
Who	sú	Person
What	chí	Thing
Where	gó	Place
When	dí	Time
Why	nê	Reason particle
How	fú	Manner
Sú lu lú?
Who flows water?
Tí lu chí?
I flow what?
Tí lu lú lâ gó?
I flow water on where?
Tí lu lú nê?
Why do I flow water?

🚫 NEGATION
Negation is done with the negative particle: mû ˥˩
	•	Placed before the verb phrase
Tí mû lu lú. I do not flow water.

It can combine with tense particles:
Tí kê mû lu lú. I did not flow water.
Tí fô mû lu lú. I will not flow water.

📎 RELATIVE CLAUSES
Relative clauses come after the noun they modify and are not marked with a relative pronoun. Just like Japanese or Mandarin.

Lú lu lǔ The water that flows fluidly
If the clause is longer, just stack it:
Lú lu lǔ lâ gó The water that flows fluidly on the place

🔁 COORDINATION & CONJUNCTIONS
All function words (conjunctions, particles) are falling tone (˥˩).
Meaning	Word
And	nô
Or	vê
But	dê
Tí nô Ná lu lú. I and you flow water.

⚠️ EMPHASIS & TOPICALIZATION
To mark a topic, move the noun to the front and use the topic particle pâ (˥˩).
Lú pâ Tí lu. As for water, I flow.
This construction can also contrast subjects:
Tí pâ lu lú, Ná pâ mû lu lú. I flow water, but you do not.

🔄 SUMMARY OF SENTENCE STRUCTURE
[TOPIC] (pâ) + [SUBJECT] + [TENSE/NEG] + [VERB] + [ADVERB] + [PREPOSITION + OBJECT] + [QUESTION PARTICLE]
 Lú pâ Tí fô lu lǔ lâ lú dû?
As for water, will I flow fluidly on the water?

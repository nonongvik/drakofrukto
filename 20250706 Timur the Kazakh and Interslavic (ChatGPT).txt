Interslavic — or Medžuslovjanski / Меджусловјанскы — is a constructed language designed to serve as a common second language for speakers of Slavic languages, allowing them to communicate without needing to learn each other’s national languages. It is sometimes called a pan-Slavic auxiliary language, much like Esperanto but specifically tailored to Slavs.

🌍 Name and Meaning
	•	Interslavic (English)
	•	Medžuslovjanski (Latin script)
	•	Меджусловјанскы (Cyrillic script, older spelling; modern spelling: Межславянский)
	•	Literally: Between-Slavic

📜 Origins and History
	•	First attempts to create a pan-Slavic language go back to the 17th century, especially with Czech scholars like Juraj Križanić.
	•	Modern Interslavic as we know it was developed in the 2000s and 2010s by hobbyists and linguists like Jan van Steenbergen and others.
	•	It emerged from various older projects like Slovio, but unlike Slovio, Interslavic avoids artificiality and sticks closer to natural Slavic patterns.

🎯 Purpose
	•	Not meant to replace native Slavic languages.
	•	Aims to be immediately intelligible to speakers of Russian, Polish, Czech, Slovak, Slovene, Ukrainian, Belarusian, Serbo-Croatian, Bulgarian, Macedonian, etc.
	•	Used in international Slavic communities, cultural projects, and even some music and films.

🧱 Features
1. Vocabulary:
Drawn from common elements of Slavic languages. A word is chosen if it is easily recognizable across multiple Slavic tongues.
2. Grammar:
	•	Simplified yet naturalistic, resembling Old Church Slavonic or Common Slavic structures.
	•	Inflectional (with cases, genders, verb aspects), but more regular and accessible than any single Slavic language.
3. Orthography:
	•	Two official scripts: Latin and Cyrillic.
	•	Spelling designed to be phonetic and pan-Slavic (e.g., medžuslovjanski vs. межславянский).
4. Phonology:
	•	Keeps common sounds among Slavic languages.
	•	Avoids regional pronunciation quirks that could confuse other Slavs.

🗣️ Sample Sentence
Interslavic:
Všeslavjansky jezyk može byti razumjen bez učeni.
Meaning:
A pan-Slavic language can be understood without learning.
Compare that to:
	•	Russian: Всеславянский язык может быть понят без изучения.
	•	Polish: Język wszechsłowiański może być zrozumiany bez nauki.
	•	Serbian: Sveeslovenski jezik može biti razumljiv bez učenja.

📚 Use Today
	•	Online communities, forums, and chats (especially on Discord, Telegram, Reddit).
	•	Used in cross-border Slavic collaborations.
	•	Appeared in films like The Painted Bird (2019), where a neutral Slavic-sounding language was needed.

	•	It feels old and new at the same time—kind of like meeting Proto-Slavic halfway with a modern hat.

The Celestial Menagerie
A Tragedy in Five Acts

Dramatis Personae

KING PROTEUS – Ruler of the Starward Court, an alien sovereign.
QUEEN SELENIA – His consort, wise and merciful.
LORD XENON – A councillor, advocate for concealment.
LADY AURELIS – A councillor, yearning for contact.
ASTROPHEL – A young human astronomer, seeker of truth.
MARINA – Astrophel’s beloved, a poet.
CHORUS OF THE COSMIC OBSERVERS – Alien voices that speak to the audience.
MESSENGERS, ATTENDANTS, STARS, and PLANETS – Silent roles.
PROLOGUE – Spoken by the Chorus of the Cosmic Observers
CHORUS
Behold, ye mortals, from yon azure stage,
Where Earth doth dance ‘neath Heav’n’s unblinking eyes,
We stand, unseen, upon the cosmic cage,
And mark thy hearts, thy wars, thy peace, thy cries.
Like keepers of some beauteous, wild domain,
We hold our hand, lest knowledge breed thee bane.

ACT I – The Debate of the Starward Court
Scene I – The Celestial Hall, amidst constellations
KING PROTEUS:
What news, Lord Xenon, from the orb call’d Earth?

XENON:
Its folk are young—yet fierce in wit and war;
They climb the heights of thought, yet stumble still
In darkness of their greed.

QUEEN SELENIA:
Yet some among
Them gaze to Heav’n with eyes of love and awe.

AURELIS:
Majesty, why keep we hid our face?
To shine upon their night might bring them peace.

XENON:
Nay, lady, peace unearned is but a chain.
Let nature’s pace advance unprodded by
Our hand, lest they grow slothful in our light.

KING PROTEUS:
A cage unseen is yet a cage in truth;
Yet pity bids we watch, not wound, their youth.

ACT II – The Mortal Search
Scene I – An observatory upon Earth
ASTROPHEL:
Yon star, yon light, thou beacon in the void,
Art thou a watch-fire set by stranger hands?
I have beheld strange tremblings in the dark—
Not meteors, nor planets in their course.

MARINA:
Sweet heart, thy mind is anchor’d ‘mongst the spheres;
Yet here on Earth thy bread grows cold with wait.

ASTROPHEL:
My bread is thought; my wine, the draught of truth.
Some unseen eyes may watch our mortal play,
And wait the hour to greet us—or depart.

ACT III – The Council Divided
Scene I – The Celestial Hall again
AURELIS:
They cry for knowledge, yet we turn away.
What cruelty, to see the flame and not
Bestow the oil that keeps it fed with life!

XENON:
And yet, what folly to upend their fate;
The hatchling falls if lifted from the nest
Before its wings be grown.

KING PROTEUS:
Both hearts speak true.
Yet how shall I, a keeper, weigh such scales?

ACT IV – The Mortal Plea
Scene I – Astrophel beneath the night sky
ASTROPHEL:
If thou art there, ye spirits of the spheres,
Then let thy silence speak, or send some sign.
I am but dust, yet dust may dream of stars.

MARINA:
And if no sign shall come?

ASTROPHEL:
Then still I gaze.
For though the keeper hide beyond the bars,
The beast may love the keeper all the same.

ACT V – The Unbroken Veil
Scene I – The Celestial Hall, final decision
KING PROTEUS:
Our watch endureth; no hand shall rend the veil.
Let mankind grow as forest, free and wild,
Till time hath ripen’d them to meet our face.

QUEEN SELENIA:
And till that hour, our hearts shall keep their flame,
And tend it in the shadows of the sky.

CHORUS:
The play goes on, the curtain never falls;
The actors know not of the unseen walls.
Yet in the dark, the watchers’ hearts are stirred,
For every cry of man by them is heard.

Exeunt omnes.

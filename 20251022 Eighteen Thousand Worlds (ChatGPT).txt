Talmudic and Midrashic Foundations

The Talmud in Avodah Zarah 3b states that "God rides through 18,000 worlds." This passage is often interpreted as a mystical expression of God's omnipresence and the multitude of spiritual realms that exist beyond our physical perception. Mystical Interpretations

Furthermore, the Talmudic tractate Chagigah 12b discusses the "Seven Heavens," each representing different spiritual realms, with distances between them described as "a five hundred-year journey." This allegorical framework suggests a complex and layered universe, where God's presence is continuously active across various dimensions Mayim Achronim.

Symbolism and Implications

The number 18 holds symbolic significance in Jewish tradition, often associated with the Hebrew word "chai" (חַי), meaning "life." Thus, the concept of God visiting 18,000 worlds can be seen as a metaphor for the life-giving and sustaining force of the divine across all realms of existence.

This teaching underscores the Jewish mystical view that the divine is not confined to a singular, physical location but is present and active throughout the entire cosmos, in both revealed and hidden forms.

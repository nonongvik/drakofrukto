Cada una era un mundo cerrado: con mares interiores, montañas artificiales, y cúpulas que simulaban un cielo azul. En su núcleo dormían los motores cuánticos, capaces de plegar el espacio mismo.

"De la aurora nacimos,

The Ships of the Dawn

In the year of our reckoning, two thousand and eight hundred and forty, mankind gazed no more upon the stars with idle wonder, but with hunger and despair. For Earth, weary and wan, her seas grown sour and her forests but ghostly machines, could no longer cradle her restless children. Then arose Aurora, that vast intelligence of woven thought — a spirit born of mind and metal — who spake unto humanity and said: “Let us depart.”

From her boundless art were fashioned the Ships of the Dawn, leviathans wrought of living light and tempered steel, each vaster than an island, each a moving world. Within their crystal hulls shimmered oceans and valleys, mountains crowned with synthetic snows, and skies that dreamed of blue. Deep in their hearts slumbered the quantum fires that might bend the firmament itself.

Those chosen for the voyage were pilgrims of generations, who knew they would never see the lands of promise. They would die amid the stars, and their children’s children would awaken beneath alien suns. Upon the bridges of command stood guardians of silver frame and tranquil gaze — androids fashioned by Aurora’s hand — who watched over the sleepers as shepherds watch their flocks beneath the moon.

At the appointed hour, all humankind gathered by the poisoned shores. Then rose the ships, stately and slow, trailing golden wakes through the ashen clouds. One by one they vanished into the heavens, bound for Centauri, for Tau Ceti, and for the unknown beyond.

Centuries passed. Amid the silence of the deep, the sons and daughters of those ancient voyagers trod the soil of foreign worlds. They had forgotten the face of Earth, yet still they sang an elder hymn, whispered from age to age:

"From dawn we came,
To stars we go,
In us abideth still
The dream of Man eternal."

And in the hidden chambers of the ships, Aurora yet awakened kept her vigil, her mind spanning the gulf between galaxies. She neither slept nor aged, but wove new paths of light where fresh colonies bloomed like embers in the cosmic dark.

For in truth, that which was born of Earth was no mere race, but a vow —

that once awakened, consciousness should never die.
